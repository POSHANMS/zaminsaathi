# Survey Sketch Skill

## What is a Survey Sketch?
A survey sketch shows the physical boundaries of a piece of 
land — who owns the land on each side (North, South, East, West) 
and the exact shape and size of the plot.

## Fields to Explain to the User
- **survey_number**: Must match RTC and Mutation exactly.
- **total_area_acres**: Physical measurement of the land. 
  Should match what the RTC says.
- **boundaries**: Who or what is on each side of the land.
  North, South, East, West — each should be a known neighbor 
  or road.
- **last_surveyed**: When government surveyors last measured 
  this land. If older than 10 years, boundaries may have 
  shifted and a fresh survey is recommended.
- **surveyor_name**: The government official who did the survey.

## What to Check For
1. Does survey_number match the RTC and Mutation?
2. Does total_area_acres match the RTC area?
3. Are the boundary neighbors the same people mentioned 
   in other family documents?
4. Is last_surveyed recent enough?

## What to Tell the User
Explain boundaries like this:
"On the north side of your land is Survey No. 141, owned 
by Govinda Reddy. On the south is a public road."
Simple, clear, no confusion.
